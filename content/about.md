Dr. Shalu Chopra is a media and mass communication scholar, researcher, and columnist. Her
work moves easily between the university and the public conversation. She is a
doctorate-qualified academic with more than eight years in higher education. She teaches,
researches, and writes about how media shapes belief, from misinformation and viral video to
advertising, ethnography, and the craft of storytelling. Her teaching spans advertising,
communication, development communication, personality development, and media research.

Her doctoral research, completed at Amity University in 2024, studied the social impact and
credibility of fake viral videos on social media. That work now extends into her forthcoming
book, *Manipulative Realities: Viral Propaganda of Digital Deception*. She also holds an MSc
in International Marketing Management from Manchester Metropolitan University. It adds a strong
grounding in digital branding, consumer behaviour, and the creative industries to her
communication scholarship.

Before academia, she trained inside the media industry. She hosted a news show for a YouTube
channel and completed internships across television and radio, including production at
Doordarshan, outside broadcasting at Red FM, and All India Radio. That practitioner's
instinct still shapes her teaching and writing: she is as comfortable decoding an algorithm as
she is in front of a camera or microphone. As the "Prism" columnist for The Present Minds, she
turns research-informed insight into writing for a broad, general readership.

She has published in Scopus-indexed and UGC-recognised journals, presented at international
conferences, and serves as India's Country Coordinator for the World Conference on Media and
Mass Communication (MEDCOM). Throughout, her aim is simple: research that is rigorous and
humane, and that does not stay on the shelf.
