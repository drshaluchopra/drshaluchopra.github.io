Marketing & Brand Projects

Strategic marketing and brand projects from Dr. Chopra's MSc in International Marketing Management at Manchester Metropolitan University.

Marketing Launch Campaign: Campfire x Quorn

Manchester Metropolitan University · Group Project

A strategic launch campaign for Campfire, built on the existing brand equity of Quorn. Working in a cross-functional team, Dr. Chopra helped design an integrated go-to-market strategy and present it in a simulated client-style pitch to the Campfire team.

Brand strategyCampaign planningConsumer insightTeamworkClient presentation

Key contributions
  • Developed a brand extension and positioning strategy for Campfire under the Quorn umbrella.
  • Conducted market and consumer insight analysis to identify target segments and growth opportunities.
  • Designed an integrated marketing mix (product, price, place, promotion) and a launch roadmap.
  • Created a campaign narrative and creative concept aligned with Quorn's brand values.
  • Delivered an in-person pitch simulating a real agency and client environment.

Destination Branding Campaign: Brussels

Manchester Metropolitan University · Module: Branding International Places & Destinations · Group Project

A people-led destination branding campaign for Brussels, repositioning the city from an institutional, EU-focused image to a human, multicultural, and community-driven cultural destination. Dr. Chopra's key contribution was the core experience model and strategic framework, including the "Fill Your Cart" personalisation concept and the use of the ATLAS framework to put the brand strategy into action.

Place brandingDestination marketingExperience designStrategic frameworksCollaborative strategy

Key contributions
  • Conceptualised "Fill Your Cart", a 1 to 5 day personalised cultural journey model co-created with local ambassadors.
  • Applied the ATLAS (Think, Listen, Act, Speak) framework to turn brand strategy into actionable destination initiatives.
  • Helped define Brussels' new positioning: "The Culture Between Cultures".
  • Conducted PESTLE and SWOT analysis to justify and validate the people-led branding approach.
  • Designed ambassador-led experiences, neighbourhood activation, and cultural micro-experiences.
  • Linked the campaign to Brussels' 2030 cultural vision for long-term relevance, and delivered a professional group pitch.

Development Communication: Social-Change Campaigns

In the Development Communication module, the goal is real societal change. Dr. Chopra guides her students to design and run field campaigns in the communities that need development most, turning theory into action.

  • Education access

    7 Kadam (Seven Steps)

    A social-impact campaign supporting children from economically weaker sections who still lack access to quality education, despite government schemes. Over seven consecutive days, Dr. Chopra's students gave free lessons in English, Mathematics, and Hindi, teaching children at times that suited them, morning or night, to strengthen their basic academic skills. The sessions were documented and shared as an online campaign to inspire wider community involvement.

    Outcome: Reached over 60,000 accounts across digital platforms, driving strong engagement and showing how small, consistent efforts can create real educational opportunities for underserved children.
